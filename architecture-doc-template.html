RiskImpactMitigation

Risk 2 — Short name Describe what goes wrong and when. Concrete mitigation steps. Include any one-time setup vs. ongoing process.
Risk 3 — Short name Describe what goes wrong and when. Concrete mitigation steps.
Risk 4 — Cost overrun Ad-hoc or poorly optimized workloads inflate costs beyond projections. Cost alerts at [service]. Monthly review comparing actual vs. estimated. Usage attribution to specific consumers/teams.
Risk 5 — Key-person dependency Only one person understands the full system. All infrastructure as code (reproducible). All logic version-controlled (documented). Runbooks for every operational procedure. Pair during build phases.
Risk 6 — Silent failure A component fails and no one notices until a downstream consumer is affected. Freshness / health monitoring with alerts when expected output hasn't arrived within SLA window. DLQ depth monitoring. End-to-end smoke tests on a schedule.

[Unified approach] over [dual-path ambiguity]

The prior proposal presented [two approaches] as co-equal without designating a primary or clarifying when to use each.

[Agnostic architecture] over [tool lock-in]

The prior proposal built the architecture around a specific [tool / vendor] and its specific requirements.

Why we chose tool-agnosticism: [Specific concern that motivated this — cost, capability gap, strategic flexibility.] All [business logic / metric definitions / rules] live in [canonical location] (version-controlled, portable), and the [tool] is a thin layer that connects via [standard protocol]. This means the team can evaluate, switch, or supplement the [tool] without re-architecture.

The prior proposal defined N phases with acceptance criteria but no explicit decision gates or timelines.

Why we chose decision-gated phases: A team cannot commit to a multi-phase roadmap without checkpoints. Each gate includes specific evaluation criteria — [example criteria] — that tie technical milestones to business outcomes.
